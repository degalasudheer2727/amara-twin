The capital at a glance

One plan. Nine cities. Endless data.

Indicative headline figures drawn from public reporting on the Amaravati master plan. Every number in AMARATWIN is sourced and framed as indicative until verified against official APCRDA data.

The four pillars

A national-grade open-data nexus, localised for Amaravati.

Modelled conceptually on proven national open-data platforms — adapted into four pillars that turn a master plan into a public, governed, intelligent data utility.

The data layer · 12 connectors

Every domain of the city, as a live connector.

Each connector publishes governed, refreshable feeds with a defined schema, owner and cadence — the API surface of the digital twin.

The spatial layer · 9 theme cities + Quantum Valley

A polycentric capital, zone by zone.

The master plan is organised into specialised theme cities. In AMARATWIN each is a 3D zone with its own connectors filtered in.

The platform layer

From raw signal to citizen experience.

A four-stage reference architecture moves data from source systems to governed, public-facing experiences.

Built for everyone

One twin, three lenses.

The same governed data, rendered for the people who use it differently.

The hybrid mobile app

Carry the capital in your pocket.

The AMARATWIN companion app — built with Flutter — puts the live twin, every connector and your neighbourhood's data on iOS and Android. Bilingual, offline-aware, accessible by default.
